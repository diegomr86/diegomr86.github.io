Dev for Nature

2021
Socioenvironmental Developer

From the end of 2018 until now, I have been developing, in partnership with Terrakrya, several technology projects focused on the preservation and recovery of the environment and traditional peoples. During this same period, I continued with the free software projects, improving and creating new features.

Check out some of the projects I have actively participated in:

Cultivar Brasil: web and mobile application in Portuguese focused on Brazil that was developed with the aim of facilitating learning about the cultivation of food by itself.

Rede Agroflorestal: collaborative knowledge building platform on Agroforestry

Redário - Rede de sementes: management system for the collection, processing, storage, planting planning and sale of seeds for the recovery of degraded areas.

Biobá: platform conceived as a multi-sector strategy to create connections between agroecological and extractive production (sociobiodiversity), generated from family farmers and traditional communities and indigenous peoples with consumer markets, within a logic guided by basic principles of sustainability, agroecology, transparency, participation and social control and solidarity economy.

Caminhos da Semente: network of people and organizations aiming to scale up ecological restoration in Brazil with a focus on the direct seeding method.

Associação Floresta Protegida (AFP): Indigenous organization of the Mẽbêngôkre - Kayapó Indigenous People representing approximately 3,000 indigenous people from 31 villages located in the Kayapó, Mekragnoti and Las Casas Indigenous Lands, in the south of the state of Pará-Brazil. AFP was created in 1998 and works through the development and execution of strategic projects, in 4 action lines: Culture and Knowledge, Productive Activities and Income Generation, Environmental and Territorial Monitoring and Institutional and Political Strengthening.

Xingu+: network formed by the villages and communities that are part of the Xingu Corridor, cultural, linguistic and ecological heritage.
